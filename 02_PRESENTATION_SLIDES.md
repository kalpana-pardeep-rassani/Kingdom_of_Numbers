# The Kingdom of Numbers - Presentation Flow

## Presentation Goal

Create a light, easy-to-follow walkthrough of the app that shows:
- what problem it solves
- how the learning journey works
- why students stay engaged
- what makes the app practical and useful

Tone: simple, friendly, clean, and confident.

---

## SLIDE 1: TITLE

### Main Content
- **The Kingdom of Numbers**
- Story-based math learning for Grade 6-8 students
- **By:** Kalpana Pardeep
- **Course Name:** Vibe Coding & AI Agents
- **Instructor:** Zeeshan Saleem

### Visual Direction
- Full-width hero slide
- Strong title typography
- World icons: 🏝️ 🌲 📚 🏛️

### Speaker Flow
Start with the idea that math practice can feel more like an adventure than a worksheet.

## SLIDE 2: THE IDEA

### Main Content
- Many students find math repetitive and stressful
- This app turns practice into guided game missions
- The goal is to make learning feel clear, active, and rewarding

### Visual Direction
- Left: short problem statement
- Right: simple before/after comparison
  - textbook-only practice
  - story-based guided practice

### Speaker Flow
Keep this slide short. Move quickly from the problem to the app idea.

## SLIDE 3: HOW THE APP WORKS

### Main Content
1. Sign up or log in
2. Take a 10-question diagnostic test
3. Get recommended practice
4. Play through world-based levels
5. Track progress and achievements

### Visual Direction
- Use a horizontal flow with 5 steps
- Add small icons for each step

### Speaker Flow
Explain the app as a smooth journey instead of a long feature list.

---

## SLIDE 4: SMART START WITH DIAGNOSTIC

### Main Content
- The app checks integers, fractions, algebra, and geometry
- It finds weak and strong topics
- It recommends where the student should begin

### Visual Direction
- Use a screenshot of the world recommendation or results screen
- Highlight: score, weak topics, recommended order

### Speaker Flow
Emphasize personalization. This is where the app becomes more than a simple quiz.

## SLIDE 5: THE FOUR LEARNING WORLDS

### Main Content
- **Integers Island**: gains, losses, number line thinking
- **Fractions Forest**: parts, decimals, percentages
- **Algebra Academy**: patterns, expressions, equations
- **Geometry Garden**: shapes, angles, perimeter, area

### Visual Direction
- Show 4 clean cards in one grid
- Each card gets one emoji, one topic line, one short description

### Speaker Flow
Show that the worlds are fun, but still clearly based on syllabus topics.

## SLIDE 6: WHY IT FEELS ENGAGING

### Main Content
- Story-based questions instead of dry textbook wording
- Character dialogue and mission-style prompts
- Instant feedback after every answer
- XP, stars, streaks, and achievements

### Visual Direction
- Use gameplay screenshot
- Overlay 4 small callout labels on the image

### Speaker Flow
Focus on balance: fun is there to support learning, not distract from it.

## SLIDE 7: LEARNING THAT STAYS VISIBLE

### Main Content

Explain how visibility helps motivation.

## SLIDE 9: KEY TAKEAWAY

### Main Content
- The Kingdom of Numbers turns math practice into a guided adventure

## Design Notes

- Keep each slide visually open
- Prefer short phrases over paragraphs
- Use the same colors, spacing, and card style throughout
- Keep transitions smooth by letting each slide naturally answer the last one
- Use screenshots only where they help explain the flow quickly
