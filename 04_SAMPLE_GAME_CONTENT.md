# Kingdom of Numbers - Federal Board Content Design

## Purpose

This document shows how the app now converts Federal Board Pakistan Mathematics content for Grade 6 to 8 into guided game missions without losing the original learning objective.

## Updated World Structure

### 1. Integers Island
- Federal Board topic cluster: Integers, directed numbers, comparison, addition, subtraction, multiplication
- Grade focus: 6-7
- Learning arc: number line -> gains and losses -> signed operations -> multi-step reasoning

### 2. Fractions Forest
- Federal Board topic cluster: Fractions, decimals, equivalent fractions, percentage
- Grade focus: 6-8
- Learning arc: equal parts -> operations with fractions -> decimals -> percentage applications

### 3. Algebra Academy
- Federal Board topic cluster: Algebraic expressions, substitution, linear equations, patterns
- Grade focus: 7-8
- Learning arc: expression meaning -> substitution -> one-step equations -> two-step equations -> word problems

### 4. Geometry Garden
- Federal Board topic cluster: Lines, angles, triangles, quadrilaterals, perimeter, area
- Grade focus: 6-8
- Learning arc: shape facts -> angle facts -> perimeter -> area -> reasoning with properties

## How Textbook Questions Become Game Questions

### Rule 1: Keep the chapter skill unchanged
- If the book is testing integer subtraction, the game still tests integer subtraction.
- The story only changes the setting, not the mathematics.

### Rule 2: Add a mission context
- Book style: Calculate -12 - 7.
- Game style: A submarine scout is at -12 meters. A whirlpool pulls it down 7 more meters. What is the new depth?

### Rule 3: Explain in student language
- Explanations avoid abstract jargon when a simple step-by-step sentence works better.

### Rule 4: Balance fun and clarity
- The mission should create interest.
- The wording should still let a student identify the exact math operation.

## Improved Sample Questions

### Integers Sample
- Chapter: Integers
- Skill: Add a positive integer to a negative integer
- Story question: Your climber starts 4 meters below the camp bridge and climbs 9 meters up the rope path. What is the climber's new position relative to the bridge?
- Correct answer: 5 m
- Why it works: The story gives a visual model for negative and positive movement without hiding the operation.

### Fractions Sample
- Chapter: Fractions
- Skill: Add unlike fractions
- Story question: To heal the ancient tree, the wizard pours 1/2 vial of moonwater and 1/4 vial of rainwater. How much liquid is poured in total?
- Correct answer: 3/4
- Why it works: The fantasy layer is light, while the student still has to find a common denominator.

### Algebra Sample
- Chapter: Linear Equations
- Skill: Solve a two-step equation
- Story question: The clock tower resets only if 2m + 3 = 15. What value of m resets the clock tower?
- Correct answer: 6
- Why it works: The mission gives purpose, but the equation remains fully visible.

### Geometry Sample
- Chapter: Area
- Skill: Find the area of a rectangle
- Story question: The royal carpet room needs a floor cover for a rectangle 7 m by 5 m. What is the area of the floor?
- Correct answer: 35 square m
- Why it works: Students still apply the exact textbook formula, but inside a practical scenario.

## Full Chapter Transformation Example

### Chapter Chosen
- Federal Board topic: Integers
- Grade coverage: Grade 6 and Grade 7 foundations

### Chapter Objectives
- Represent positive and negative numbers
- Compare integers on a number line
- Add and subtract integers in context
- Apply sign rules in multiplication

### Textbook to Game Conversion Model

#### Textbook Objective 1
- Book style: Identify the integer that represents 3 degrees below zero.
- Game mission: The weather tower marks temperatures below zero for the night watch. If the temperature is 3 degrees below zero, which integer represents it?
- Learning preserved: interpreting negative values

#### Textbook Objective 2
- Book style: Find -4 + 9.
- Game mission: Your climber starts 4 meters below the camp bridge and climbs 9 meters up the rope path. What is the climber's new position relative to the bridge?
- Learning preserved: adding a positive integer to a negative integer

#### Textbook Objective 3
- Book style: Find -12 - 7.
- Game mission: A submarine scout is at -12 meters. A whirlpool pulls it down 7 more meters. What is the scout's new depth?
- Learning preserved: subtracting integers in context

#### Textbook Objective 4
- Book style: Evaluate (-4) x (-5).
- Game mission: A magic mirror reverses a debt of 4 coins across 5 portals. What is (-4) x (-5)?
- Learning preserved: multiplication sign rule

#### Textbook Objective 5
- Book style: Compare -9 and -4.
- Game mission: The rescue drone must choose the colder weather station first. Which temperature is colder: -9 C or -4 C?
- Learning preserved: comparing integers on a number line

## Difficulty Design

### Easy
- Goal: build recognition and confidence
- Typical tasks: identify, represent, direct calculation

### Medium
- Goal: apply the concept in a realistic mission
- Typical tasks: one-step word problems, basic reasoning, connected operations

### Hard
- Goal: combine the concept with decision-making
- Typical tasks: multi-step reasoning, rule application, comparison, interpretation

## Tone and Feedback Guidelines

- Character language should motivate without insulting the learner.
- Feedback should point to the skill, not just the final answer.
- Story should support the concept, not distract from it.
- Explanations should be short enough to read after every attempt.

## Resulting Design Standard

The app should feel like this:
- Educational enough that a teacher can map it to the chapter objective
- Fun enough that a student wants to keep going after one question
- Clear enough that the story helps understanding instead of hiding the math
